About Me

My name is Andy Barron, and in May 2015, I received a BS in computer science from the University of Virginia. My extensive résumé sets me apart from other fresh college graduates, and I have learned valuable skills at each of my jobs and internships.

Four years as webmaster for my university's music department taught me how to work closely with clients to meet their needs and how to manage and maintain a large multi-user web system. Two years as a teaching assistant for Introduction to Computer Science, Topics in Computer Vision, and Database Systems taught me clear communication and management skills (and a great deal of patience). And two summer internships at NASA's Langley Research Center have honed my research and collaboration skills.

My programming ability is stronger than any other recent graduate's. I am fluent in Python, Java, JavaScript, shell scripting, and the up-and-coming Rust systems language. I also have strong skills in C, C++, C#, SQL, Ruby, PHP, and Swift. On the Web side, I have extensive experience with HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, and Node.js - both from academic classes and personal projects. I am a relentless when debugging code, and I have extensive experience designing, developing, troubleshooting, and polishing all kinds of software systems - object-oriented and otherwise.

My programming skills do not end there, though. I am very quick to pick up new programming languages, and I am self-taught in more than half of those that I've mentioned. My passion for software development has led me to create many side projects during my undergraduate career. Combined with my extensive software development experience and strong collaborative skills, I am absolutely the ideal candidate for any software engineering or research position.

As an aspiring developer, I firmly believe that I would be a great fit for any software engineering position. I would like nothing more than the chance to prove this to you.

Thank you very much for taking the time to read my profile on Hired. I hope to hear from you soon!
